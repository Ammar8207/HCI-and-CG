Biography

This site introduces key Human–Computer Interaction ideas with a focus on Don Norman’s design principles and the GOMS and KLM models. It serves as a course hub for exploring usability, task analysis, and how people interact with interfaces.

Don Norman is a pioneer in the field of Human–Computer Interaction, known for his work on user-centered design, usability, and cognitive engineering. His writing and research connect how people think with how interfaces should behave, arguing that good design makes actions discoverable, feedback clear, and errors easy to recover from.

He has helped shape modern interaction design by framing products as systems of goals, actions, and feedback. The concepts you will study in this course—Norman’s Action Cycle, GOMS task structures, and KLM timing operators—provide a shared vocabulary for analyzing how people perform tasks and why interfaces succeed or fail.
