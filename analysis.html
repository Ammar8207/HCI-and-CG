Overview

This analysis compares how Norman’s Action Cycle and GOMS explain the same task: enabling Wi‑Fi on a smartphone. The goal is to see where the models align and where they deliberately differ.

  • Norman: explains intention, execution, and evaluation.
  • GOMS: structures expert tasks into goals and methods.
  • KLM: estimates execution time for low‑level actions.

Norman’s Human Action Cycle

Norman’s Human Action Cycle describes how people turn intentions into actions and then evaluate results. The cycle begins with forming a goal and planning what to do, followed by specifying and executing the action. After acting, users perceive system feedback, interpret what it means, and evaluate whether the goal was achieved. Good interfaces reduce the Gulf of Execution (making actions easy to figure out) and the Gulf of Evaluation (making feedback clear and meaningful).

Automatic Syringe (Action Cycle Example)

In an automatic syringe, the user’s goal is to deliver the correct dose. The Gulf of Execution appears if the controls do not make the next safe action obvious. The Gulf of Evaluation appears if feedback does not clearly confirm dose completion or errors.

Key Terms

Gulf of Execution
The gap between a user’s goal and the actions available in the interface.
Gulf of Evaluation
The gap between system feedback and the user’s ability to interpret it.
Method (GOMS)
A procedure that accomplishes a goal using a sequence of operators.
Operator (GOMS/KLM)
A basic action such as a keystroke, tap, or pointing movement.

Study Tip

How to compare models quickly

Ask whether the model explains thinking and feedback (Norman), task structure (GOMS), or execution time (KLM).
